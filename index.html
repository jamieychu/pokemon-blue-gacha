Pokémon Blue · Gacha Design

Trainer Recruitment

Gotta Recruit 'Em All!

Pokémon Blue's endgame has a shelf life problem — beat the Elite Four and there's nothing left to chase. Trainer Recruitment adds a gacha collection system that extends play by 25–50 hours without breaking the premium model that makes the game work.

Product Challenge

Gacha systems usually depend on real-money spending and FOMO mechanics — limited banners, premium currency, daily login pressure. None of that works here. Pokémon Blue is a finished box product with no live service layer and no monetization infrastructure.

So I built the whole system on a single earned currency, tied to things players are already doing — beating trainers, catching Pokémon, exploring routes. The pull has to feel exciting on its own — no pressure to spend, just good design.

Key Design Decisions

🪙

Earn, Never Buy

No premium currency, no real-money path. Players earn Recruit Pts entirely through gameplay — gym badges, trainer battles, Pokédex milestones — so the system rewards exploration, not spending.

🛡️

Transparent Safety Net, Hidden Magic

Hard pity (guaranteed Gym Leader at 35 pulls) and rare pity (at 10 pulls) are always visible — players can see the safety net. Soft pity (gradually increasing rates from pull 20) stays hidden, so the thrill of "getting lucky" is real even when the math is helping.

🎨

Cosmetic Only, Zero Power Creep

Trainers are visual reskins — they change who you play as, not how the game plays. The collection stays worth chasing without ever touching game balance.

Design Process

Three concepts were explored before landing on Trainer Recruitment:

✕
Character Customization Sprite fidelity too low to read cosmetic differences on Game Boy hardware.
✕
Pokémon Variants Risked breaking the core catch-and-battle loop that drives the whole game.
✕
Limited-Time Banners Would require live service infrastructure and paid currency — converting a box product into something it isn't.
✓
Trainer Recruitment Works within existing Game Boy sprite constraints and taps the game's most underused player motivation: being someone other than Red.

Prototype follows original Pokémon Blue visual systems and Game Boy conventions. Liberties taken with sprite fidelity, text character count, and screen dimensions.
